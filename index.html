SOCRATES

Socrates was a classical Greek philosopher who is considered one of the founders of Western philosophy. He is known for his method of questioning, now called the Socratic method, which involves asking a series of probing questions to stimulate critical thinking and illuminate ideas. Despite not writing any philosophical works himself, his ideas and teachings were recorded by his students, including Plato, and continue to influence philosophy today.

PLATO

Plato was an ancient Greek philosopher, a student of Socrates, and the teacher of Aristotle. He founded the Academy in Athens, one of the earliest institutions of higher learning in the Western world. Plato is known for his works that explore justice, beauty, equality, and the nature of reality, particularly through dialogues featuring his teacher Socrates.

ARISTOTELES

Aristotle was an ancient Greek philosopher and polymath, a student of Plato and teacher of Alexander the Great. He made significant contributions to numerous fields including metaphysics, ethics, politics, biology, and logic, laying the groundwork for much of Western intellectual tradition. His works have profoundly influenced both ancient and modern thought across various disciplines.
